Featured Projects

Some things I've built recently

IoT Air Quality Monitoring System

An IoT air quality monitoring system based on Master–Slave architecture with ESP32 nodes communicating via LoRa SX1278. The Master actively polls the Slave using a request/response protocol with CRC8 validation to ensure data integrity. Both nodes run FreeRTOS for multitasking and TinyML for on-edge environment classification. The Master aggregates data and pushes it to Supabase. A Next.js dashboard displays real-time data, renders heatmaps using IDW interpolation, and provides historical charts with AQI gauges. Custom PCB designed for sensor integration.

ESP32 LoRa FreeRTOS TinyML PCB Design Supabase Next.js

Arduino Line Following Robot

An autonomous line-following robot powered by Arduino with a PID control algorithm for precise speed and steering. The chassis is custom-designed and 3D-printed, with a self-designed PCB layout for the control board. Features an IR sensor array and motor driver for smooth, high-speed line tracking.

About Me

Get to know me & my skills

I'm a motivated and curious Engineering undergraduate at Da Nang University of Science and Technology (DUT), based in Da Nang, Vietnam. My journey in technology began with a deep fascination for how hardware and electronics power the modern world.

I specialize in embedded systems and IoT development — designing firmware for microcontrollers, building sensor-driven solutions, creating custom PCB layouts, and developing smart devices that bridge the physical and digital worlds. I'm experienced with platforms like MCU (ESP32, Arduino, STM32), FPGA, and RASPBERRY PI, and I work with real-time operating systems like freeRTOS to build reliable, multitasking embedded applications.

My long-term vision is to become a Hardware Engineer and an expert in embedded systems and IoT architecture. I aspire to work at multinational technology companies where I can contribute to cutting-edge products, collaborate with world-class engineering teams, and push the boundaries of what connected hardware can achieve.

I believe that the future lies at the intersection of intelligent hardware and software — and I'm committed to becoming a specialist who can design, prototype, and deliver production-ready embedded solutions. Whether it's optimizing low-power sensor networks, deploying TinyML models at the edge, or engineering robust communication protocols, I'm always eager to take on new challenges and grow.
